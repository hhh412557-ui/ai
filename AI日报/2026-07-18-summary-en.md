## [First Atmosphere Found on Rocky Exoplanet in Habitable Zone](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy4kdd1e0ejo) ⭐️ 8.0/10

JWST has confirmed the presence of an atmosphere on LHS 1140b, a rocky super-Earth in the habitable zone of a red dwarf star 48 light-years away. This is the first definitive detection of an atmosphere on a rocky planet in a habitable zone around a red dwarf. This discovery challenges the long-held assumption that red dwarfs' intense stellar activity would strip atmospheres from close-in rocky planets, suggesting such planets may be more common than thought. It also provides a prime target for future biosignature searches. LHS 1140b is about 5.6 times Earth's mass and 70% larger in radius, placing it in the super-Earth category; refined data suggest it may be an ocean world with 9–19% water by mass. JWST emission spectroscopy ruled out a mini-Neptune interpretation, confirming a relatively thin atmosphere.

hackernews · neversaydie · Jul 17, 14:06 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48947560)

**Background**: Red dwarfs are cooler and smaller than the Sun, so their habitable zones are much closer, exposing planets to intense stellar flares and particle winds that can erode atmospheres. Until now, no rocky planet in such a zone had been confirmed to retain an atmosphere. LHS 1140b was discovered in 2017 by the MEarth Project and has been a key target for atmospheric studies due to its transiting nature.
