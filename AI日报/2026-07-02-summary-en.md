## [First Synthetic Cell That Grows and Divides Created](https://www.quantamagazine.org/for-the-first-time-a-cell-built-from-scratch-grows-and-divides-20260701/) ⭐️ 10.0/10

Researchers led by Dr. Kate Adamala at the University of Minnesota have created SpudCell, the first synthetic cell built from scratch that can grow and divide. The achievement was announced on July 1, 2026, and marks a major milestone in synthetic biology. This breakthrough demonstrates that a minimal synthetic cell can perform the fundamental life processes of growth and division, bringing us closer to understanding the origins of life and enabling future applications in biotechnology, medicine, and materials science. It also challenges the definition of life itself. SpudCell lacks a cytoskeleton and divides using a unique mechanism involving membrane remodeling rather than the protein-based machinery of natural cells. The work was initially rejected by the journal Cell, with one reviewer claiming SpudCells are not real biology, leading Adamala to release the manuscript directly to journalists before peer review.

hackernews · defrost · Jul 1, 14:20 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48747304)

**Background**: Synthetic biology aims to build artificial cells from non-living components to understand life's fundamental principles. Previous efforts achieved feeding, growth, and DNA replication in synthetic cells, but cell division remained a major hurdle because natural division relies on complex cytoskeletal rearrangements. Adamala's team bypassed this by designing a simpler division process.
