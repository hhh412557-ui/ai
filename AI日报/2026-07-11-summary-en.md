## [GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra Proves 50-Year-Old Math Conjecture](https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2075643450196971805) ⭐️ 10.0/10

OpenAI announced that its GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra model produced a proof of the Cycle Double Cover Conjecture, a 50-year-old open problem in graph theory, using 64 subagents in under one hour. This marks the first time an AI has autonomously proven a long-standing mathematical conjecture, demonstrating advanced multi-agent reasoning and potentially transforming how mathematical research is conducted. The proof was generated by GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra's subagent architecture, which spawns 64 specialized subagents to work on different parts of the problem. OpenAI released both the prompt and the full proof for public review.

twitter · OpenAI · Jul 10, 18:08

**Background**: The Cycle Double Cover Conjecture, posed by Tutte, Itai, Rodeh, Szekeres, and Seymour, asks whether every bridgeless graph has a collection of cycles that covers each edge exactly twice. It has been open for about 50 years and is a central problem in graph theory. GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra is OpenAI's latest model featuring a subagent mode that decomposes complex tasks into parallel subtasks.

## [Apple Sues OpenAI for Trade Secret Theft](https://9to5mac.com/2026/07/10/apple-sues-openai-trade-secret-theft/) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Apple has filed a lawsuit against OpenAI, accusing the company of orchestrating a systematic scheme to steal trade secrets by recruiting former Apple employees and instructing them to conceal their moves. This lawsuit could severely damage OpenAI's reputation and business relationships, especially in hardware development and enterprise trust, potentially reshaping the competitive landscape of the AI industry. Apple alleges that OpenAI recruits, including a Mr. Tan, emailed themselves confidential information upon leaving Apple and used Apple's hardware secrets to approach suppliers. The lawsuit also claims OpenAI instructed new hires to avoid telling Apple about their new jobs to prolong access.

hackernews · stock_toaster · Jul 10, 20:47 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48865019)

**Background**: Trade secret lawsuits between tech giants are rare but can have severe consequences, as seen in the Waymo vs. Uber case that ended Uber's self-driving project. Apple has a history of aggressively protecting its intellectual property, and this case involves allegations of a coordinated effort to steal hardware and software secrets for AI development.

**Discussion**: Commenters largely view the evidence as damning, predicting a severe outcome for OpenAI similar to the Waymo vs. Uber case. Many express concern that OpenAI's behavior undermines enterprise trust, with some advising businesses to avoid using OpenAI models due to IP risks.
